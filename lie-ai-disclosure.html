The AI Coach is an educational and personal development tool designed to help adult men:

5. Limitations of AI-Generated Content

You should be aware of the following limitations when using the AI Coach:

  • Accuracy. AI-generated responses may contain factual errors, misinterpretations of your input, or recommendations that are not appropriate for your specific situation. The AI Coach is not infallible.
  • Context. The AI Coach does not know your full personal history, medical background, or current circumstances beyond what you share in conversation. Its responses are based on limited information.
  • Emotional understanding. The AI Coach uses pattern recognition to interpret your messages. It does not experience emotions and may fail to recognize the full emotional weight or nuance of what you are communicating.
  • Crisis detection. While the AI Coach includes safety protocols designed to detect and respond to expressions of distress or self-harm ideation, these protocols are not guaranteed to catch every instance. If you are in crisis, do not rely on the AI Coach — contact a qualified professional or emergency service immediately.

When the AI Coach’s crisis safety protocol is triggered, it will:

  • Stop the current coaching conversation;
  • Acknowledge your distress without judgment;
  • Clearly state that it is an AI and cannot provide emergency or clinical support;
  • Display the crisis resources listed in Section 6; and
  • Encourage you to contact a trusted person or professional immediately.

The AI Coach cannot call 911 on your behalf, cannot stay with you after the session ends, cannot verify whether you have contacted a crisis resource, and cannot follow up with you after a safety event. These are inherent limitations of an automated system.

When the AI Coach’s crisis safety protocol is triggered during a conversation, the Company logs the occurrence of the trigger event, the timestamp, and the AI Coach’s safety response. This logging is required for compliance with California SB 243 and is described in further detail in the Privacy Policy. These logs are retained for a minimum of three (3) years and do not include the content of your conversation.

  • Independent judgment. You are responsible for evaluating AI Coach outputs and making your own decisions. The AI Coach’s responses are suggestions and frameworks for reflection, not directives.

6. If You Are in Crisis

If you are experiencing suicidal thoughts, self-harm ideation, or a mental health emergency, please contact one of the following resources immediately:

  • 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline: Call or text 988 (available 24/7)
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 (available 24/7)
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
  • SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 (available 24/7)
  • Emergency services: Call 911

If you are outside the United States, go to https://blog.opencounseling.com/suicide-hotlines/ to look for the suicide hotline in your country.

These resources are also available in the AI Coach’s Help section at all times. If the AI Coach’s safety protocol detects distress in your conversation, it will provide these resources directly within the conversation.

Beginning July 1, 2027, as required by California SB 243, the Company will publish annual reports on how often crisis safety protocols were triggered and how they performed. These reports will not identify individual users.
