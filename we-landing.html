What Proxi We is

A coaching tool for the in-between.

Proxi We is a conversational AI coaching tool built by Dr. John Schinnerer and Joree Rose — a psychologist, executive coach, and couples therapist who are also partners in a real, imperfect, evolving relationship. It's not a conflict resolution app. It doesn't give you scripts. It responds the way they would: naming the cycle rather than assigning blame, asking one question that creates space, offering a reframe that helps both of you see what's actually happening.

Most couples don't break down in therapy. They break down on a Tuesday night when something small lights the familiar fuse. They go quiet for three days after an argument neither of them can fully reconstruct. They drift — not dramatically, but steadily — in the weeks between appointments when no one is holding them to the work.

Proxi We is built for those weeks. For the moment before it escalates. For both of you, together or separately, whenever you need a frame.

How it works

Three moves, every conversation.

1
Either or both of you describes what's happening
The argument that hasn't been fully repaired. The week of distance neither of you has named. The conversation you keep almost having. One partner or both can bring it — Proxi We will hold the space for what's real.
2
Proxi We names the cycle — not the villain
It takes no sides. It doesn't pathologize either partner. It identifies the dynamic — the pursuer-withdrawer dance, the defensive spiral, the way one person's anxiety activates the other's shutdown — and asks the one question that might interrupt it.
3
You leave with a reframe and a next step
Not a verdict. Not a to-do list. One concrete move: a way to re-enter the conversation, a repair attempt, a thing to say or notice or try before the next time this particular pattern runs.

What you'll work on

Four areas where couples get stuck.

Communication & Conflict Patterns
Why conversations escalate so fast. The cycles that run on autopilot. How to say the hard thing without triggering the familiar spiral.
Repair & Reconnection
The distance that builds after an argument. How to come back. Repair attempts that actually land. Getting back to the baseline rather than letting the residue compound.
Intimacy & Emotional Connection
The drift into roommate mode. What closeness actually requires. The gap between physical and emotional intimacy — and how to close it before it becomes habitual.
Partnership, Growth & the Long Game
Wanting the same things at different times. Staying differentiated while staying connected. Building a relationship that grows instead of slowly contracting.

An honest answer

"Shouldn't we just
go to couples therapy?"

If you're in serious pain — yes. A skilled couples therapist is irreplaceable for deep conflict, attachment wounds, or the kind of hurt that's been compounding for years. We'd tell you to go.

Proxi We is for a different moment.

Most couples don't break down in therapy.
They break down on a Tuesday night.

It's 10pm and the conversation that started fine is going sideways. It's the third week in a row you've both been distant and neither of you has named it. It's the familiar cycle you both see coming — and still can't seem to interrupt before it's already run.

Proxi We is built for that gap. Not to replace therapy. To hold the work between sessions. To give you a frame in the moment when you need one — before the pattern has fully run, or while you're still trying to understand what just happened.

It was built by John and Joree — a psychologist and a therapist who are also in a real partnership. They know what the science says about couples. They also know what it actually feels like from the inside, including the parts that don't resolve neatly. Proxi We doesn't take sides, doesn't pathologize either partner, and doesn't pretend the work is easy. It just holds the space for both of you to see the cycle more clearly.
