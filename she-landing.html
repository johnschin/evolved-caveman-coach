What Proxi She is

A warm, reflective space — available at any hour.

Proxi She is a conversational AI coaching tool built on the frameworks and approach of Joree Rose — licensed therapist, mindfulness teacher, and author. It's not a journaling app. It doesn't offer generic self-care tips. It responds the way Joree would: reflecting back what you've said, asking the question that helps you hear yourself more clearly, and offering a path forward that's grounded in real practice.

Many women find it easier to be fully honest in a space with no social stakes — no wondering how you'll be perceived, no managing someone else's reaction. Proxi She is that space. Built for the 2am moment when your mind won't quiet, the morning when the same loop is running again, and the steady practice of turning toward yourself rather than away.

This is not therapy. It's not a substitute for professional mental health care. It's a coaching and mindfulness tool for women who are ready to develop a deeper, more compassionate relationship with their own inner experience.

Built on real work

Joree Rose, MA LMFT

Joree is a licensed marriage and family therapist, mindfulness and meditation teacher, retreat leader, and author of three books including A Year of Gratitude and Mindfulness, It's Elementary. She has helped thousands of people build greater self-awareness, emotional resilience, and a more compassionate inner life — through her private practice, her podcast Journey Forward, and her teaching.

How it works

Three moves, every conversation.

1
You bring what's actually alive for you
You don't need a clear question. You don't need to arrive polished. Sometimes the most useful starting place is: "I'm not sure where to start, but here's where I am right now." Proxi She will help you find the thread.
2
Proxi She reflects, asks, and offers a path forward
Not advice for advice's sake. A real response: what's being said underneath what you said, one question that slows you down in a good way, a reframe that opens something, and a next step that honors where you actually are.
3
You build a practice of returning to yourself
Mindfulness isn't a technique. It's a habit of attention. Proxi She is where that practice lives — in the daily or weekly conversations that build, over time, into something that actually changes how you move through your life.

What you'll work on

Four areas, one through line.

Emotional Awareness & Self-Compassion
Learning to name what you're feeling before it runs you. Building a kinder, more honest relationship with your own inner experience.
Anxiety, Stress & Overwhelm
The racing mind at 2am. The chronic low-grade hum of too much. Mindfulness-grounded approaches to finding steadiness inside uncertainty.
Relationships & Boundaries
The patterns that keep showing up. Learning to ask for what you need. Saying no without guilt. Being in relationship without losing yourself.
Meaning, Identity & Self-Trust
The quiet hunger for more presence in your own life. Rebuilding trust in your own knowing. The question of who you are when you strip back what you're supposed to be.

An honest answer

"Shouldn't I just
work with Joree directly?"

If you can — yes. Working with Joree one-on-one is a different kind of experience. She brings attunement, intuition, the full presence of a skilled therapist who can hold all of you over time and respond to what's underneath what you're saying.

Proxi She is not Joree. That matters, and she'd want you to know it.

Awareness doesn't wait for the next appointment.
Neither should you.

It's 2am and your mind is turning over something you can't quite name. A conversation earlier left a residue — unsettled, unresolved. You had a moment of real clarity this morning, the kind that slips away before you can do anything with it. You need a quiet space to think without managing what anyone will think of you for thinking it.

That's where Proxi She lives. It's built on Joree's body of work — her frameworks as a therapist and mindfulness teacher, her approach to self-compassion and emotional awareness. When you bring what's actually happening, Proxi She slows you down in the best way: asking the question that helps you hear yourself more clearly, offering a reframe, and giving you something real to carry forward.

Either way: the work is worth starting. The only question is where you begin.
